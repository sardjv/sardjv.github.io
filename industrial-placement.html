Software Developer - Industrial Placement

An opportunity for a student or graduate to complete their industrial placement in a supportive environment.

Software is changing all the time, so we understand that nurturing and learning from the next generation of developers is a must! If you are looking for an industry placement in programming or software development and are happy to work hard, laugh a lot, and learn new skills, then SARD is for you.

We are a small IT company based in London/Canterbury and we are offering an industry placement to students on Computer Science (or similar) degrees. We provide a variety of software to the NHS and our client list is growing all the time, meaning there is plenty of work and we are recruiting!

What will I be doing?

Your main duties will include the day-to-day maintenance of SARD, including technical support, bug fixes and creating/updating features of the system. We have a Technical Support rota for when the Customer Support Team receive tech queries on our busy live chat system. The work is varied and flexible, and there will be plenty of opportunity for creativity in your workload.

We're looking for someone to spend their year with us striving to make a positive impact on our clients' lives, and leaving with a wealth of knowledge, confidence and personal growth.

This is a full-time position, working Monday to Friday, 09:00 to 17:00.

For further information please email recruitment@sardjv.co.uk. You can find more information about SARD on our website: www.sardjv.co.uk.

Person Specification:

  • Proactive work ethic and independent thinker: we are a team and we are here to help each other. But once you're up and running you should feel comfortable to plan your own working day and prioritise your projects and work accordingly. Someone will always be only a phone call or cup of tea away!
  • Strong interpersonal skills and ability to communicate: we are a close-knit bunch and the whole company has a short daily 'scrum' so we can catch up on the day to day business of each team. Our Marketing Director is always looking for new blog posts for our website, so if you have discovered something interesting within the tech world or something you're working on for example, this is definitely encouraged. Within the tech team we all chat online/in person regularly in order to share knowledge and skills or ask questions and get help. Don't be afraid to ask.
  • Problem solving capabilities: that's what programming is - puzzles and problem solving! We don't expect you to know everything about everything, just need you to enjoy the process of learning, creating and programming. We have a fantastic tech team who are from a variety of different backgrounds and skills, but we all unite in our love for code.
  • Passionate about learning new skills: as mentioned, software is constantly changing. We need someone who wants to learn and is humble enough to do so. We all learn from each other all the time, not just in coding but the complexities of our system and how doctors and nurses use it each day.
  • A dog lover: we have an office puppy, Fig. He is super friendly and very affectionate, but will demand a pat or a cuddle from you every now and then, so it would help if you weren't allergic or scared. He has his own SARD branded jacket.

Technical specification:

Essential:
  • Good understanding of the web and fundamental web technologies
  • Strong understanding of HTML/CSS/JavaScript
  • Experience working with Ruby on Rails - although no expertise is required. You should have a grasp of some OO language (Java, Python, C++ etc). Additional learning will be achieved on the job.
    Try these CodeAcademy courses: Learn Ruby on Rails, Learn Ruby
Desirable:
  • Experience with/knowledge of SASS, HAML, Backbone.js is a plus
  • Experience with/knowledge of MongoDB/Mongoid is a plus

Sounds great! How do I apply?

Please e-mail recruitment@sardjv.co.uk with an up-to-date CV and a covering letter detailing why you think you'd be suitable for this placement.

We are a small company, which means we all have to work closely together. Therefore it would be useful if you include some information about your personal interests and activities in your covering letter (this does not need to be related to the advertised job, so feel free to be honest!). We want to get to know you!

Closing date for applications: Friday 1st March at 12 noon.
